# Rules of Injera Be Wat

## Objective

Collect cards during play. Points are counted only after the circle is empty, and the player with the highest point total wins.

## Setup

Shuffle two standard 52-card decks together and add exactly two Jokers, for 106 cards total. Place four cards face up in the **Middle** and the remaining 102 face down in the circular draw pile. Each player has a locked side area for collected cards.

## A turn

Pick any one face-down card from the Circle. Use it to make one capture:

- **Match:** select one Middle card with the same number or rank.
- **Add:** select two or more numeric Middle cards whose total equals the picked card. Ace counts as 1. Face cards cannot add.
- You may capture several separate valid groups with the same picked card. For example, a picked 9 can take a Middle 9, 8 + Ace, and 4 + 5 together.
- **Pile match:** if the picked card has the same rank as the top card of your own collected pile, press equals to add it directly to your pile.
- **Take a pile stack:** if the picked card matches an opponent's visible top card, select that pile to take every consecutive card of that rank from its top. Deeper cards remain locked.

Players must spot captures themselves. The game does not reveal available answers. A player may end their turn even after missing a capture; the picked card then joins the Middle, play passes clockwise, and there is no take-back. A successful capture includes the picked card and lets that player pick again.

Every player's top collected card is visible. A newly picked matching card may join your own pile or take the matching stack from the top of an opponent's pile. Cards below that top matching stack stay locked.

## Joker

There are exactly two Jokers. A picked Joker immediately collects every card in the Middle and every card collected by every opponent. Cards still in the Circle remain there. The Joker is also collected, and play continues unless it was the final Circle card.

## End and scoring

The game ends after the last Circle card is resolved. The player who picked that final card also collects every card still in the Middle.

- Ace and number cards: **1 point each**
- Jack, Queen, and King: **10 points each**
- Joker: **1 point each**

Equal highest totals produce joint winners.
